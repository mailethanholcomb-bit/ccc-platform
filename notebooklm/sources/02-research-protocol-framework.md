# CCC 30-Minute Research Protocol Framework

## Overview

The 30-Minute Research Protocol is a structured, AI-powered due diligence engine that evaluates acquisition targets across four sequential phases. It uses the Claude API to perform rapid business intelligence gathering and analysis, producing a comprehensive risk assessment in under 30 minutes.

## Phase 1: Industry Research

The first phase provides a macro-level understanding of the target's operating environment.

### Analysis Dimensions
- **Industry Overview**: 2-3 paragraph assessment of the industry landscape
- **Market Size**: Estimated total addressable market with reasoning
- **Growth Trends**: Historical trajectory, key growth drivers, and headwinds
- **Competitive Landscape**: Competitor density, barriers to entry, and market fragmentation in the target's specific geography
- **Seasonal Patterns**: Revenue and demand cyclicality
- **Industry Risks**: Regulatory threats, technology disruption potential, macroeconomic vulnerabilities

### Key Principle
Industry research is geography-specific. A plumbing company in Austin, TX faces different competitive dynamics than one in rural Montana. The protocol always contextualizes findings to the target's location.

## Phase 2: Digital Footprint Analysis

This phase assesses the business's online presence and reputation.

### Assessment Areas
- **Website Audit**: SSL certificate, mobile responsiveness, last update date, quality score (1-5)
- **Google Reviews**: Review count, average rating, sentiment analysis, trend direction (improving/declining/stable), flagged review themes
- **Social Media**: Platform presence, activity level, follower estimates, content quality
- **Digital Footprint Score**: Overall score from 1-5 (5 = excellent)

### Why It Matters
Digital presence is a leading indicator of business health. A business with no website, poor reviews, or inactive social media often signals operational neglect — a potential acquisition opportunity if the buyer can improve digital marketing, or a red flag if the decline reflects deeper problems.

## Phase 3: Distress Signal Detection

The most critical phase for identifying hidden risks and understanding the seller's true motivation.

### Distress Signals
Each signal is classified by severity:
- **High**: Deal-threatening issues requiring immediate attention
- **Moderate**: Concerns that need investigation but may be manageable
- **Low**: Minor issues that can likely be addressed post-acquisition

### Analysis Categories

**Succession Indicators**
- Owner age and retirement timeline
- Existence of a succession plan
- Key person risk (how dependent is the business on the owner?)
- Management team readiness for transition

**Operational Fatigue Markers**
- Owner workload assessment (hours per week)
- Staffing adequacy relative to revenue
- Process maturity (systematized vs. owner-dependent)
- Burnout indicators

**Financial Stress Signals**
- Revenue stability and trajectory
- Margin pressure indicators
- Customer concentration risk
- Cash flow concerns

**Owner Motivation Analysis**
A 2-3 paragraph deep analysis of the seller's stated reason for selling versus their likely true motivation. Understanding why an owner is really selling is often the most valuable insight in due diligence.

## Phase 4: Scorecard and Verdict

The final phase synthesizes all prior research into a weighted acquisition scorecard.

### Weighted Scorecard (100 points max)
| Criterion | Weight | Max Score |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Financial Fundamentals | 30% | 30 |
| Market Position | 20% | 20 |
| Digital Presence | 15% | 15 |
| Operational Stability | 20% | 20 |
| Owner/Transition Risk | 15% | 15 |

### Verdict Rules
- **GO**: Total score >= 70 — proceed with acquisition
- **CONDITIONAL**: Total score 50-69 — proceed with caution, address concerns
- **NO-GO**: Total score < 50 — do not pursue

### Deliverables
- Weighted scorecard with justification for each score
- Protocol verdict (GO / CONDITIONAL / NO-GO)
- Executive assessment (2-3 paragraphs)
- Case study narrative (3-5 paragraphs) suitable for investment committee presentation

## Execution Model

Phases 1-3 run in parallel for speed (they are independent of each other). Phase 4 runs after all three complete, as it synthesizes their outputs. This parallel execution keeps total analysis time under 30 minutes.
